Japanese Curry Katsu

Description

Katsu curry is a favorite dish in Japan, featuring a heaping plate of rice with flavorful Japanese curry, which tends to be sweeter and more mild in flavor than Indian curry, and topped with tonkatsu. Deep-fried tonkatsu and chicken katsu taste amazing slathered in curry sauce.

Ingredients

  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil
  • Onion, finely chopped
  • 1/2 carrot, grated
  • 620ml water
  • 1 packet S&B Golden Curry Sauce Mix Mild 92g
  • 4 pork cutlets
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 50g plain flour
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 100g panko breadcrumbs
  • Vegetable oil, for deep frying
  • Cooked rice, to serve
  • Select all ingredients

Steps

  1. Heat the 1 tablespoon of oil in a large saucepan.
  2. Fry the onion and carrot over a medium heat until softened.
  3. Add the water, bring to a boil, reduce the heat and simmer for 10 minutes.
  4. Remove from the heat then add the curry roux, in pieces, stir until completely dissolved.
  5. Simmer gently for anoter 5 minutes, stirring constantly.
  6. Season both sides of the pork cutlets with salt and pepper. Lightly coat them with the flour, dip in the beaten egg and then coat with the breadcrumbs.
  7. Heat oil in a large heavy-bottomed saucepan to 180Â°C. Deep fry the meat until golden and cooked through. Drain on kitchen papers.
  8. Slice the meat and serve with the curry sauce drizzled over the cooked rice.
